## Introduction

In a music industry obsessed with manufactured authenticity and algorithmic virality, **Allyson Glado** stands as a beacon of genuine artistic expression. She's not just making reggae-pop music—she's redefining what it means to be an authentic artist in the contemporary landscape.

Based in Paris and collaborating with trumpeter Serigne Diagne, Allyson creates a revolutionary sound that honors reggae's conscious roots while embracing modern sensibility. Her music is designed not for chart positioning or TikTok virality, but for genuine human connection.

## Who Is Allyson Glado?

Allyson Glado is a reggae-pop artist who refuses to fit into conventional categories. Her work blends:

- **Reggae grooves** — The heartbeat of Caribbean consciousness
- **Pop sensibilities** — Modern accessibility without compromising depth
- **Soulful vocals** — Emotional vulnerability and power
- **Deep lyricism** — Poetry that addresses what society leaves unsaid

She's not trying to be the next big thing. She's too busy being authentically herself.

## The Sound: Reggae-Pop Fusion Done Right

### Not Pop with Reggae Sprinkled On

Allyson's reggae-pop isn't pop music with reggae elements added for flavor. It's a genuine fusion where:

1. **Reggae is the foundation** — The one-drop rhythm, the consciousness, the Caribbean soul
2. **Pop is the architecture** — Modern production values, global accessibility, contemporary relevance
3. **Soul is the connective tissue** — Emotional authenticity binding it all together

### The Production Philosophy

Working with arranger and trumpeter Serigne Diagne, Allyson elevates reggae-pop into orchestral territory. His trumpet work isn't decoration—it's essential to the emotional narrative. His arrangements respect reggae traditions while pushing contemporary boundaries.

The result: Music that sounds contemporary without feeling trendy. Music that honors reggae heritage while speaking to today's listeners.

## Musical Influences: A Spectrum of Authenticity

### Reggae (Caribbean Roots)
Bob Marley's consciousness. Peter Tosh's revolutionary spirit. The one-drop rhythm that makes your body move and your soul listen. This is where Allyson's foundation lives.

### Pop (Modern Accessibility)
Not dumbing down, but democratizing. Pop's superpower is reaching people. Allyson uses this to deliver profound messages in accessible packages.

### Soul (Emotional Vulnerability)
Aretha Franklin's power. Marvin Gaye's intimacy. Soul teaches the importance of emotional authenticity. You can't fake it.

### Jazz (Harmonic Complexity)
Modal jazz's freedom. Charlie Parker's invention. Jazz teaches collaboration and risk-taking.

### Urban (Contemporary Reality)
Hip-hop's poetry. Grime's directness. Urban music's refusal to sanitize reality. Contemporary voice.

Together, these influences create a sonic palette that's simultaneously rooted and revolutionary.

## The Serigne Diagne Collaboration

### Beyond Session Musician

Serigne Diagne isn't a hired hand on Allyson's tracks. He's an artistic partner whose contribution extends beyond musicianship:

**Trumpet Work:**
His horn arrangements elevate reggae grooves. The trumpet isn't playing over the reggae—it's having a conversation with it. Sometimes answering, sometimes questioning, sometimes leading.

**Arrangements:**
Jazz-influenced harmonic movement that respects reggae's rhythmic foundation. Complexity that serves the song, never overshadows it.

**Artistic Direction:**
Serigne's vision shapes Allyson's sound. Their collaboration produces something neither could create alone.
